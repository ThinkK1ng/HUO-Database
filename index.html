Stats and Attributes

All level 1 characters begin with 1 point in each stat. Races are capable of affecting stat distribution.

Strength: determines physical attack power.
Intelligence: determines how much MP a character has.
Cunning: determines Corruption attack power.
Vitality: determines how much HP a character has.
Agility: determines movement speed, flexibility, and reaction speed.
Mind: determines magic and faith attack power.
Endurance: determines stamina and defense.
Luck: increases item drop rate by 0.5% per point. Maxes at 50%.

Each skill in the game carries a certain damage type. The following list of damage types are known as attributes:
Fire: Strong against Ice and Nature, weak to Water and Earth, while mutually beneficial to Wind and Lightning.
Water: Strong against Fire, weak to Earth and Lightning, while mutually beneficial to Ice and Nature.
Wind: Strong against Nature, Ice, and Lightning, weak to Earth, while mutually beneficial to fire.
Nature: Strong against Lightning and Ice, weak to Fire and Wind, while mutually beneficial to Water and Earth.
Earth: Strong against Fire, Physical, Wind, and Water, weak to Ice and lightning, while mutually beneficial to Nature.
Ice: Strong against lightning and Earth, weak to Fire, Lightning, Wind, and Nature, while mutually beneficial to Water.
Lightning: Strong against Ice, Earth, Physical, and Water, weak to Wind, Ice, and Nature, while mutually beneficial to Fire.
Physical: Weak to Earth and Lightning but neutral to the other attributes.

Three special attributes exist outside of the purview of the other attributes. They are strong against all of them and weak to none.

Faith: Strong against Corruption and weak to Magic.
Corruption: Strong against Magic and weak to Corruption.
Magic: Strong against Faith and weak to Corruption.

The final attribute is special in the sense that it does no damage, but its use can be blocked by Faith, Corruption, and Magic attribute skills.

Recon: Weak against Faith, Corruption, and Magic. Allows the user to detect people and objects at range.

Currency

The economy of HUO revolves around eXcel, or "X" as it is commonly reffered as. It is the only currency within the world of HUO aside from item trading.
Many players seek to acquire as much eXcel as possible for one simple reason: eXcel can be converted into real world currency at a rate of two eXcel per dollar.
This implementation from the devs ensured that simply playing HUO could lead to real world benefits, thus cementing its spot as the top game on all the charts.
